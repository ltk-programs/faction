# About FACTION

## What is FACTION?

FACTION is a primary-source evidence index. It collects, organises, and presents official documents, verified footage, court records, and government reports on topics of public significance — without editorial interpretation, spin, or commentary.

The premise is simple: before debating what something means, everyone should be looking at the same documents. FACTION provides those documents.

## The problem FACTION addresses

Public discourse about contested events is frequently shaped by secondhand accounts, partisan framing, and selective citation. When someone makes a claim about what "the evidence shows," they often mean "a journalist's summary of the evidence" or "an activist group's characterisation of the evidence." The underlying documents are rarely shared.

This is a solvable problem. Most significant events produce a substantial primary source record — court filings, agency investigations, official testimony, verified footage. These documents are public. They just aren't organised in a way that makes them easy to find, compare, or cite.

FACTION organises them.

## What FACTION is not

**FACTION is not a news outlet.** It does not report on events. It documents them after primary source records exist.

**FACTION is not a fact-checker.** Fact-checkers assess claims made by politicians or public figures. FACTION indexes the source documents that everyone can use to check claims themselves.

**FACTION is not an advocacy organisation.** It has no position on any topic it covers. The same evidentiary standard applies regardless of which political direction a document's conclusions point.

**FACTION is not a debate arbiter.** It does not declare winners or losers in contested factual disputes. When primary sources genuinely conflict, FACTION presents both positions with their supporting documents.

## Who runs FACTION?

FACTION is an independent project. It has no institutional affiliation, no political alignment, and no external funding. All sourcing decisions are made against the methodology criteria published on this site.

Corrections and source suggestions from the public are welcomed via the [submission form](/submit). All submissions enter an editorial review queue and are accepted or rejected based solely on whether they meet the tier criteria.

## Scope

FACTION covers topics where:
1. A substantial primary source record exists or is actively being created
2. The factual questions are of meaningful public interest
3. The documentary record is navigable (not so voluminous as to be impossible to index)

FACTION currently focuses on the United States, where government document disclosure practices create the most accessible primary source record. International coverage expands as capacity allows.
