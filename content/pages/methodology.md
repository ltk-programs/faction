# Methodology

FACTION exists to establish a shared evidentiary baseline — the same documents, the same footage, the same official record — before debate begins. This page explains exactly how that works.

---

## What FACTION publishes

FACTION publishes **primary sources only**. A primary source is a document, recording, or dataset produced directly by the institution or party involved in the event — not a journalist's report about it, not an analyst's interpretation of it.

Examples of primary sources FACTION accepts:
- Government agency reports and regulations (EPA, DOJ, FBI, CDC, etc.)
- Court filings, verdicts, sentencing documents, and dockets
- Official legislative records (congressional hearings, floor votes, CRS reports)
- Peer-reviewed studies published in indexed academic journals
- Official autopsy and medical examiner reports
- Body camera and surveillance footage verified by the releasing agency
- Corporate regulatory filings (SEC, OSHA, etc.)
- Official press statements from verified institutional accounts

Examples of what FACTION **never** publishes:
- News articles, op-eds, or analysis pieces (even from credible outlets)
- Social media posts (even from official accounts, unless Tier 3 criteria are met)
- Secondhand summaries of primary sources
- Leaked documents of unverified provenance
- Opinion pieces, editorials, or commentary
- Content from think tanks or advocacy organisations, regardless of ideological alignment

---

## Source tiers

Every piece of evidence on FACTION is assigned a tier.

### Tier 1 — Verified official source

The gold standard. Tier 1 sources are produced by a government agency, court, peer-reviewed journal, or recognised institutional body. They are stable (permanently archived), attributable, and authenticated. Tier 1 evidence appears without a disclaimer on FACTION because the sourcing chain is unbroken.

**Examples:** DOJ indictment, EPA regulatory filing, CDC MMWR report, court verdict form, congressional hearing transcript.

### Tier 3 — Community-verified source

Tier 3 sources are primary in nature — they directly record or document the event — but do not originate from an official institution. They are always displayed with a **Tier 3** badge and a note explaining why they are included and what has been verified about their authenticity.

**Examples:** Bystander video confirmed by police reports, official social media posts from verified accounts, documents released by parties to litigation.

There is no Tier 2. The distinction between official and community-sourced is binary. A news article quoting a government document is not a primary source regardless of the outlet's reputation.

---

## Status definitions

Each fact file carries a status that reflects the current state of the evidentiary record.

**Open** — The record is incomplete. Key evidence has not been released, trials are pending, or investigations are ongoing. FACTION presents what is currently documented, clearly marked as partial.

**Developing** — The record is substantially documented but significant events are still unfolding. New court filings, agency reports, or official actions are expected. FACTION will update the file as the record evolves.

**Resolved** — The official record is complete and the key factual questions have been answered by primary sources. "Resolved" does not mean all controversy is extinguished — it means the primary source record is stable. A resolved status can be reopened when new primary source evidence meets the reopen threshold.

---

## Timeline construction

Every timeline event on FACTION must:
1. Be attributable to a specific date documented in a primary source
2. Link to at least one Tier 1 or Tier 3 evidence ID
3. Be described in neutral, factual language — no characterisations, no adjectives implying intent

When exact times are unknown, FACTION uses the documented date only. When a date is contested in primary sources, that contestation becomes a Contested Claim entry rather than a timeline event.

---

## Contested claims

The Contested Claims section appears only when two **primary sources** directly contradict each other on a specific factual question. It is not a "both sides" feature — it is a record of genuine factual disputes in the primary source record.

To qualify as a contested claim on FACTION:
- Both positions must be supported by at least one Tier 1 source
- The dispute must be about a specific, verifiable fact (not interpretation or opinion)
- The two positions must be logically incompatible — not merely in tension

Contested claims are never manufactured to create false balance where the primary source record is clear.

---

## How fact files are updated

When new primary source evidence becomes available, the editorial team:
1. Verifies the source meets tier criteria
2. Adds the evidence to the fact file
3. Updates the timeline if applicable
4. Logs a changelog entry (visible on the public page)
5. Updates the file status if warranted

When an error is found:
1. The incorrect entry is corrected or removed
2. A **Correction** entry is logged in the changelog with a description of what changed
3. The page title carries a **CORRECTION** label for 24 hours after publication

---

## What FACTION does not do

- **Interpret.** FACTION does not explain what the evidence "means" or what conclusions you should draw.
- **Opine.** FACTION has no editorial position on any topic it covers.
- **Rate credibility.** FACTION does not assess whether a witness was telling the truth. It records what official sources say.
- **Predict.** FACTION does not forecast outcomes, verdicts, or policy changes.
- **Aggregate opinion.** Polls, surveys, and public opinion data are not published on FACTION regardless of their academic rigour.
